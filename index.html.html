About Me

Who Am I?

I'm a finance graduate who thinks in both directions at once, from a balance sheet down to the person it affects, and from a person's goals back up to the numbers that get them there. My BBA in Financial Markets at GITAM University gave me the technical foundation; leading a 150-member finance club taught me that the best analysis is worthless if you can't explain it to someone who needs it.

This fall, I'm starting my MS in Financial Analysis at the Lubar College of Business, University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee, packing up a life in Hyderabad to build one in Milwaukee, with my sights set on investment banking, asset management, or wealth management.

What actually pulls me toward this field isn't the spreadsheets. It's the moment a model stops being numbers and starts being a decision someone has to make. I like being the person who gets them there with confidence instead of guesswork.

Research

The Impact of Digitalization on Mutual Fund Distribution in India

Jan – May 2025 · GITAM University

Studied how the shift from paper-based, branch-dependent investing to mobile-first, e-KYC platforms has widened access to mutual funds across India. Ran primary research through seminars and surveys across employed, unemployed, and elderly segments, then used correlation and regression analysis to show a measurable link between digital access and new-investor growth, especially among Millennials and Gen Z.
